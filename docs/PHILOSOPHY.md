# PHILOSOPHY — The Ideas Praxeos Teaches

Organized by thinker, then by concept. Every module links back into this map.

## Carl Menger (1840–1921)

The founder. *Principles of Economics* (1871) reshaped price theory by locating value in subjective individual valuation rather than objective labor cost. His *On the Origins of Money* (1892) is the canonical account of money's emergence from commodity demand — sell-ability begets sell-ability, and the most saleable commodity becomes universal medium.

**Concepts:** marginal utility, subjective value, spontaneous institutions, origin of money.

## Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk (1851–1914)

The first Austrian to build a rigorous capital theory. His *Capital and Interest* (3 vols., 1884–1912) and *Positive Theory of Capital* establish: (a) interest arises from time preference, not from exploitation; (b) production is *roundabout*, employing intermediate capital goods that are themselves produced; (c) the length of the production structure matters — deeper roundaboutness requires more saving.

**Concepts:** capital theory, roundaboutness, positive time preference, pure-time-preference theory of interest.

## Ludwig von Mises (1881–1973)

The architect. *Die Gemeinwirtschaft* (1922, translated as *Socialism*) contained the 1920 calculation argument in fuller form — socialism cannot compute without market prices for capital goods. *Human Action* (1949) is the systematic treatise, grounding economics in the action axiom and developing praxeology as the science of human action.

**Concepts:** the action axiom, methodological dualism, economic calculation, a priori economics, monetary theory (regression theorem), business cycle.

## F. A. Hayek (1899–1992)

*Prices and Production* (1931) extended the Mises business-cycle argument into the Hayekian triangle framework. *The Use of Knowledge in Society* (1945) reframed the economic problem as one of **coordinating dispersed knowledge** — prices are the signal mechanism. *The Road to Serfdom* (1944), *The Constitution of Liberty* (1960), *Law, Legislation and Liberty* (1973–79) carry the program into political theory.

**Concepts:** knowledge problem, spontaneous order, price signals, Austrian business cycle theory, cosmos vs. taxis, rule of law.

## Murray Rothbard (1926–1995)

The systematizer. *Man, Economy, and State* (1962) is the modern textbook of Austrian economics. *America's Great Depression* (1963) applies ABCT to the 1929 episode. *What Has Government Done to Our Money?* (1963) is the pamphlet on sound money.

**Concepts:** ABCT applied, monetary history, systematic Austrian economics, anarcho-capitalism (when relevant).

## Israel Kirzner (1930–)

Student of Mises; reclaimer of entrepreneurship. *Competition and Entrepreneurship* (1973) defines entrepreneurship as **alertness to previously unnoticed opportunities for gain** — the cognitive act of *perceiving* a gap rather than the technological act of inventing.

**Concepts:** entrepreneurial alertness, market process (not equilibrium).

## Ludwig Lachmann (1906–1990)

The sophisticate. *Capital and Its Structure* (1956) emphasizes the **heterogeneity** of capital goods — they are not fungible units but specific complementary structures that form and dissolve. Expectations are subjective and dispersed.

**Concepts:** heterogeneous capital, kaleidic economy, subjective expectations.

## Hans-Hermann Hoppe (1949–)

The methodologist and political theorist. *A Theory of Socialism and Capitalism* (1989) and *Democracy: The God That Failed* (2001) on time preference under monarchy vs. democracy. *The Economics and Ethics of Private Property* (1993) on argumentation ethics.

**Concepts:** argumentation ethics, time preference applied to political regimes.

## Joseph Salerno (1950–)

The recovery scholar. His 1990 article *Ludwig von Mises as Social Rationalist* and *Money, Sound and Unsound* (2010) reconstructed the Misesian calculation argument against the Lange revisionism that had dominated textbook treatments.

**Concepts:** calculation reconstruction, sound money, Misesian method.

## Saifedean Ammous (1975–)

*The Bitcoin Standard* (2018) placed Bitcoin within the Austrian monetary tradition as a digital realization of commodity money — scarce by fiat-resistant protocol rather than by physical mining cost.

**Concepts:** sound money in digital form, stock-to-flow, monetary hardness.
