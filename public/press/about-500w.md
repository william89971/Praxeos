# Praxeos

Praxeos is an open-source library of explorable explanations for Austrian economics and praxeology. It treats economic ideas as things worth rendering with the same seriousness normally reserved for books, essays, and physical exhibitions. Each module pairs a generative or interactive surface with an essay grounded in primary sources. The result is not a teaching app in the usual sense, and not a static magazine either. It is a cultural artifact built to make difficult arguments legible without flattening them.

The first fascicle focuses on foundational Austrian themes: sound money, time preference, and economic calculation. In *The Halving Garden*, Bitcoin issuance appears as a long manuscript of block history, with new blocks entering live before they settle into the larger field. In *Time Preference Forest*, the reader manipulates patience, credit expansion, and correction to watch capital structure reveal itself visually. In *The Calculation Problem*, two economies with the same agents and technology diverge once one loses prices, making the Misesian critique visible in motion rather than left in abstract prose.

Praxeos is designed against the standard look and tone of economics communication. Too much of the field arrives as ideology, flattened pedagogy, or unattractive PDFs. This project starts from the opposite assumption: that ideas about human action, coordination, capital, and money deserve careful visual form, durable typography, and software architecture robust enough to be published, printed, and studied. The site is part argument, part library, and part workshop in how these ideas can be taught more honestly.

The project is authored by William Menjivar and built with Next.js, MDX, generative graphics, and a print-conscious editorial system. It publishes code, essays, structured metadata, RSS, Open Graph images, module-specific poster fallbacks, and a dedicated manifesto print edition. Praxeos is intended to grow in fascicles over time, with future modules extending the same method to spontaneous order, entrepreneurship, subjective value, and institutional analysis. Its wager is simple: serious ideas should be rendered seriously.
