Subject: A portfolio of explorable explanations for Austrian economics

Dear Mr Deist,

I have built an open-source library of explorable explanations for
Austrian economics called **Praxeos** (praxeos.org). The first fascicle
ships three modules — *The Halving Garden*, *The Time Preference Forest*,
and *The Calculation Problem* — each pairing a generative interactive
with a 500–1200-word primary-source-backed essay.

The site is written in a plainly Austrian register (voice guide lives
in the repo), cites mises.org generously, and is open-sourced under
MIT (code) + CC BY 4.0 (content) so translations, classroom use, and
further work by Institute staff or fellows are frictionless.

If any of it is useful for Mises Weekends, Mises Wire, or the seminars
— or if you have a particular piece of criticism — I would be glad to
hear from you.
