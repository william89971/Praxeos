Subject: Mises's 1920 argument rendered as typography

Dear Dr Klein,

I have built an open-source library of explorable explanations for
Austrian economics called **Praxeos** (praxeos.org). The third module,
*The Calculation Problem*, renders Mises's 1920 paper as a side-by-side
typographic simulation: prices emerge on the left, arbitrary recipe
choices collapse the right. A "Be the Planner" mode invites the reader
to fail the way the argument predicts they will.

The engine's core move — the planner picks recipes by hash(tick, id),
not by an optimiser — is lifted from the Salerno reconstruction of
Mises, which your scholarly work has done a great deal to keep in
currency.

If the module reads to you as faithful to the argument, I would
appreciate the endorsement. If not, I would appreciate the correction.
