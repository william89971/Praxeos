**3 / 10**

**Module 1 — The Halving Garden.**

Every Bitcoin block from genesis to tip rendered as a small botanical
organism on a Hilbert curve, arranged into four illuminated halving
epochs. New blocks appear live via mempool.space.

[attach: 10-second screen recording of the garden, zoomed]

**4 / 10**

**Module 2 — The Time Preference Forest.**

Austrian capital theory as an 18th-century woodcut. Drag time
preference and central-bank intervention. A market-correction button
plays an 8-second cascade that reveals actual capital depth.

[attach: recording of the correction cascade]

**5 / 10**

**Module 3 — The Calculation Problem.**

Mises's 1920 argument rendered as a two-panel typographic particle
system. Prices emerge on the left, arbitrary recipe choices collapse
the right.

A "Be the Planner" mode lets you try to fail the way the argument
predicts.

[attach: recording of planner mode with satisfaction gap widening]
