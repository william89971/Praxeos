Murray Rothbard was the tradition's great systematiser. *Man, Economy, and State* (1962) reconstructed the whole of catallactics as a single unified treatise in the Misesian style — a task no Austrian had attempted with comparable rigour since *Human Action* itself. The later companion *Power and Market* (1970) extended the analysis to government intervention.

Rothbard's technical economics are impeccable. His applied work is equally distinguished. *America's Great Depression* (1963) applies Austrian business-cycle theory to the 1920s-to-1932 episode with empirical detail that previous ABCT treatments had lacked. *What Has Government Done to Our Money?* (1963) is the clearest short introduction to Austrian monetary theory ever written — still the book one gives to a newcomer to explain why sound money matters.

Rothbard's political and methodological writing is separable from his technical economics and, in Praxeos, is cited more sparingly. The technical work is cited in full; the political and polemical writing is cited only where directly load-bearing on an argument.

## Key works

- *Man, Economy, and State* (1962; Scholar's Edition with *Power and Market*).
- *America's Great Depression* (1963).
- *What Has Government Done to Our Money?* (1963).
- *The Ethics of Liberty* (1982).

## In Praxeos

Rothbard's ABCT treatment informs `time-preference-forest`; his monetary work informs `halving-garden`; his systematic *Man, Economy, and State* is the background text against which much of the site's technical economics is presented.
