Saifedean Ammous is the principal contemporary Austrian monetary theorist to engage Bitcoin as money rather than as investment. *The Bitcoin Standard* (2018) places the digital asset within the same monetary tradition as Menger's origin-of-money account and Mises's regression theorem: a commodity money emerges as the most saleable good in a network, its value grounded in non-monetary prior demand, its supply governed by conditions that resist arbitrary expansion.

Ammous's technical contribution is the **stock-to-flow ratio** as the key measure of monetary hardness. A money's hardness is the ratio of existing supply (stock) to annual production (flow). Gold's stock-to-flow of roughly 62 — it would take 62 years of current mining output to double the existing stock — is the highest of any pre-Bitcoin money, and the principal reason gold served as monetary anchor across four millennia. Bitcoin's stock-to-flow rises toward infinity as the block subsidy halves, the supply schedule is cryptographically fixed, and the protocol's mining-cost mechanism couples issuance to consensus rather than to arbitrary issuance by any authority.

*The Fiat Standard* (2021) is the companion volume, extending the analytical apparatus to the twentieth-century fiat monetary regime and its consequences for time preference, capital structure, and the cost of living.
